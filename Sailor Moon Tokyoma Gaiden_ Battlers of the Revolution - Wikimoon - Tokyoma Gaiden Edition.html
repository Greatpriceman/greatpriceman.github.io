Tokyo Side Stories: Battlers of the Revolution, known as Bishouji Senshi Sailor Moon Tokyoma Gaiden: American Memories (美少女戦士セーラームーン　東京魔外伝：アメリカン・メモリーズ Bishoujo Senshi Seeraa Muun Toukyouma Gaiden: Amerikan Memoriizu) in Japan, is a video game for PlayStation 2 in the Tokyoma Gaiden Spinoff series, is loosely based on the real-world American Revolutionary War. This game's characters, as well as the Japanese name of this game, suggests that this game is a continuation of Sailor Moon Tokyoma Gaiden: Moonlight Memories, and is widely regarded as its successor.

Characters[edit | ]

Most of the characters are based on characters from the Sailor Moon Tokyoma Gaiden anime and manga, but given names and/or roles to portray a historical figure. Some characters simply have the same or similar names as their anime/manga counterparts and aren't based on historical figures. For example, the characters Barry and Metalia aren't based on historical figures named Barry, Beryl or Metalia.

Story[edit | ]

Queen Serenity/Sailor Pinako/Pinako Aino assumes the identity of the General of the American revolutionary forces, George Washington (as Georgina Washington), while Val The Csar represents the King of the British Empire and its forces as King George III.

The player assumes the role of the Revolutionary Battler, who has been summoned to aid the Americans by Luna de Lafayette, a French Military officer at Bunker Hill. However, King George III shows up and then the player decides which side he or she is going to be a part of. In order to get back home the player needs all 16 of the "Flag cards." 8 Red Flag cards are held by the British and the 8 Blue Flag cards by the Americans. The Blue Flag cards have created a defense barrier that keeps King George's forces out of North America, effectively keeping the Americans in control, however, this seal has weakened significantly.

The player can choose the Red Flag for the British, or the Blue Flag for the Americans. Each side has different opponents and different endings, but there is an option to restart the game after completion to travel the path you did not choose the first time around.

If you choose the path of hero, Val respects your choice and lets you leave in peace. After meeting Pinako and learning more about the situation, you are returned to Philadelphia, where you fight Val's minions, the British Army - Thomas Gage, Charles Cornwallis, Henry Hamilton, Henry Clinton, Benedict Arnold, and Lord Germain. All but Germain will give a card as he does not have one; Val keeps it on him at all times.

After defeating Lord North in a battle to pass time, Pinako's forces will kill him and his forces in battle, leaving you to head to London and retrieve the last Red Flag Card from Val. However, he reveals the battle to have been part of ritual, summoning Calux Demon Dragon to enact a pact to ensure his family has power. However, the player defeats the Dragon, sending him back into nothingness, defeating Val and being sent back home to the future, leaving the Flag Cards behind.

If you choose the path of selfishness in wanting to go home, you will face Pinako's friends and allies. However, unlike in the first scenario, it's basically a gauntlet - one duelist after another, no choice in who to fight. To get all the Blue Flag Cards, you face Daniel, Katerina, Liam, Kurenako, Luna, Shady, Youma Metalia, and Pinako.

Upon winning the needed cards to get home, Val is quite grateful to you and summons Calux Demon Dragon to enact a pact to ensure his family has status and wealth through the ages, and yours as well. However, it turns out to be the wrong guardian, who is extremely angry that he has been summoned after his brother was summoned and sealed away on the Moon. Defeating him to save the world, the player is thanked by Val for helping in his selfish plans, apologizing for the trouble. He gives the player a Union Jack Pendant, vowing that if it's shown to his descendants, they will do whatever they can to help, making the "Pact of the Union". Val sends the player back home and signs the Treaty of Paris, allowing Pinako to finally lower the barrier and win the revolutionary war.

In a Post-Credits scene, Four score and eleven years later, Queen Beryl is depicted as one of the Daughters of the Confederacy, along with another incarnation of Demoniser, depicted as a descendant, form the "Clan of the Demon Dragon", dedicated to spreading hatred throughout the nation, with their first act being the establishment of the "Jim Youma" laws, claiming that they'll "Put those filthy Youma in their rightful place".

Trivia[edit | ]

  • Some allusions are made to the events of Moonlight Memories by Calux Demon Dragon, before the final battle if the player sides with the British.
