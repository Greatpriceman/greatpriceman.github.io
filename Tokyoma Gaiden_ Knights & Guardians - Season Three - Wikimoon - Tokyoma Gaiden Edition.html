Tokyoma Gaiden: Knights & Guardians - Season Three refers to the third season of the Sailor Moon Tokyoma Gaiden Spin off anime (second internationally), and is considered to be the seventh season of the original English dub. this season is split into two halves, the first half deals with Pinako and her friends in varying misadventures, while the second half deals with Japan Battles (known as the Neo Warring States Tournament in the manga.)

First Half (Episodes 51-74)[edit | edit source]

the first half has five sub-stories, with the first two occurring simultaneously.

  • Daniel's Casablanca Memory (火乃ダニエルのカサブランカメモリー) : Daniel Kano must travel back to New Jersey once he hears that a member of his family is still alive. Sailor Pinako and Sailor Mercury II do not appear in this sub-story. (Episodes in this sub-story are marked with a 1)
  • Huh? Helenia?! (ね？　ヘレニア？！) : Helenia Neleton, who not only uses the name "Helen" for short, but is also a reincarnation of Sailor Stars' Good Nehelenia, is facing Pinako Aino in a battle for the ownership of Kurenai's "The King's Knights" and "Venus Transformation Pen" replica, this sub-story takes place simultaneously with the first sub-story, as Daniel Kano does not appear in it. (Episodes in this sub-story are marked with a 2)
  • Alterance - D-Point's Sailor Senshi (Ⅾ・ポイントのセーラー戦士): Pinako and her friends get Transported into an alternate version of the past, where they interrupt the fight currently taking place in The Sailor Senshi Die! The Tragic Final Battle, altering one version of the past. (Episodes in this sub-story are marked with a 3)
  • Knights & Guardians Digital Quest (Ｋ＆Ｇ：デジタルクエスト): Pinako and her friends participate in a Knights & Guardians Digital-World game to rescue Val The Csar. (Episodes in this sub-story are marked with a 4)
  • Dice Challenger (ダイス・チャレンジャー): Hikaru Kindai blames Pinako Aino for the disappearance of Delphius Z. Maxwell and tries to get revenge through his own game, Dice Challenger. (Episodes in this sub-series are marked with a 5)

During the last of these five sub-stories is the set up for the second half of the season, Japan Battles.
